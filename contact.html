Contact page changes

Part of the 19 June 2026 website update · Prepared by BrightSite

Website updatesPerformanceContact

We reworked the Contact pages across all six languages — both how they look and what they invite visitors to do. Here is what changed, and why.

What changed Now live

  • The enquiry form (“Request a quote”) now sits in a clean, clearly bounded card, so the fields are easier to read and work through.
  • The newsletter sign-up — which previously lived only in the site footer — now has its own panel directly beside the form, in your brand purple, so it stands out without competing with the enquiry.
  • Both sit on the one page, so a visitor can request a quote or subscribe without hunting around the site.
  • The same layout is applied consistently across English, French, Spanish, German, Italian and Portuguese, each using its own language’s form.

Why we did it

The Contact page is where visitor intent is highest — someone here is already thinking about getting in touch. Placing the newsletter alongside the enquiry form gives you a way to stay in contact with people who aren’t ready to request a quote yet, rather than losing them.

A sign-up tucked away in the footer is easily missed. Giving it a dedicated panel on a high-intent page makes it far more visible, while keeping the two actions side by side keeps the choice simple: enquire now, or subscribe for later.

Behind the scenes

For spam protection we moved the forms to ActiveLayer, after running into reliability problems with Google reCAPTCHA and, before that, Cloudflare Turnstile. ActiveLayer checks submissions at the server, so it keeps spam out without putting a puzzle or checkbox in front of genuine visitors. The two-column layout was built properly as part of the page structure rather than bolted on, so it holds together across screen sizes and stays straightforward to maintain.
